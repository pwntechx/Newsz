§ 00 · ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

The Geometry of Aging

Why E=1 is the only acceptable standard for a longevity protocol

Aging is not a single event. It is the accumulated expression of twelve interconnected biological failure modes — the Hallmarks of Aging — first catalogued in 2013 and expanded in 2023 to incorporate a decade of geroscience advances. Each hallmark is measurable, each is causally connected to the others, and each represents a point of nutritional or supplemental intervention.

The forensic lens applied here is E=1: every node preserved simultaneously. A longevity protocol that optimizes for one hallmark while accelerating another is not a longevity protocol — it is a trade. The geometry must hold across all twelve simultaneously or the solution is incomplete.

This analysis grades evidence at four levels: Strong (multiple peer-reviewed RCTs, consistent outcomes), Moderate (observational studies plus mechanistic evidence), Emerging (early clinical trials, strong animal data), and Speculative (mechanistic plausibility, insufficient human data). The distinction matters. All four levels appear in this document and are labeled honestly.

CORE PRINCIPLE · OVERNUTRITION VS RESTRICTION
Overnutrition activates mTORC1 + EP300 → suppresses AMPK + SIRT1/SIRT3 → disables autophagy, antioxidant defense, DNA repair.
Dietary restriction does the inverse → activates AMPK + SIRT1/SIRT3 → stimulates adaptive cellular stress responses → extends longevity in multiple organisms including primates.

Source: Cell, Hallmarks of Aging Expanded Universe, 2023

The practical implication: what you don't eat matters as much as what you do. Caloric restriction and time-restricted feeding activate the same longevity pathways that the best supplements attempt to mimic. The food protocol and the supplement stack are not additive — they are synergistic when aligned, and they cancel each other when opposed.

§ 01 · THE TWELVE HALLMARKS

Twelve Failure Modes

The complete taxonomy of biological aging — each a target, each connected

The hallmarks framework defines aging operationally: each hallmark must (1) manifest with age, (2) accelerate aging when experimentally amplified, and (3) decelerate aging when therapeutically targeted. This is the scientific standard for calling something a hallmark rather than a correlation.

HALLMARK 01
GENOMIC INSTABILITY
DNA damage accumulates faster than repair mechanisms can clear it. Dietary antioxidants and NAD+ precursors are primary nutritional interventions.
HALLMARK 02
TELOMERE ATTRITION
Chromosome end-caps shorten with each division. Omega-3 fatty acids show the strongest dietary association with telomere length preservation.
HALLMARK 03
EPIGENETIC ALTERATIONS
Gene expression patterns drift from their youthful state. Methyl donors — folate, B12, choline — are foundational dietary interventions.
HALLMARK 04
LOSS OF PROTEOSTASIS
Protein quality control fails — misfolded proteins accumulate. Autophagy inducers: spermidine, fasting, caloric restriction.
HALLMARK 05
DISABLED MACROAUTOPHAGY
The cellular recycling system degrades. Spermidine and urolithin A are the most evidence-backed nutritional autophagy inducers.
HALLMARK 06
DEREGULATED NUTRIENT SENSING
mTOR, AMPK, IGF-1, SIRT pathways lose calibration. Dietary restriction and resveratrol directly modulate these sensors.
HALLMARK 07
MITOCHONDRIAL DYSFUNCTION
Energy production degrades. NAD+ precursors (NMN/NR), CoQ10, urolithin A, and PQQ are the primary nutritional interventions.
HALLMARK 08
CELLULAR SENESCENCE
Damaged cells stop dividing but persist, secreting inflammatory signals. Quercetin and fisetin are the most studied dietary senolytics.
HALLMARK 09
STEM CELL EXHAUSTION
Tissue regenerative capacity declines. Protein adequacy and specific amino acids are dietary requirements for stem cell maintenance.
HALLMARK 10
ALTERED INTERCELLULAR COMMUNICATION
Signaling between cells degrades. Anti-inflammatory diet — Mediterranean pattern — is the most evidence-backed dietary intervention.
HALLMARK 11
CHRONIC INFLAMMATION
Low-grade systemic inflammation accelerates all other hallmarks. Omega-3s, polyphenols, and curcumin are primary dietary anti-inflammatories.
HALLMARK 12
DYSBIOSIS
Gut microbiome composition shifts toward pathogenic patterns. Fiber, fermented foods, and polyphenols are the foundational dietary interventions.
§ 02 · CORE LONGEVITY FOODS

The Food Architecture

Fifteen foods that address multiple hallmarks simultaneously — verified ground only

The strongest dietary pattern for longevity is not a single food but a constellation. Harvard's 2025 Nature Medicine study — the most comprehensive dietary longevity analysis to date — found that diets rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, unsaturated fats, nuts, and legumes, with moderate dairy and reduced processed meat, were most strongly associated with healthy aging across multiple biological markers. Every food below earned its place by addressing at minimum two hallmarks with moderate or strong evidence.

EXTRA VIRGIN OLIVE OIL
POLYPHENOL SOURCE · FAT-SOLUBLE NUTRIENT CARRIER
STRONG EVIDENCE

The most evidence-backed single food in longevity science. Oleocanthal acts as a natural COX inhibitor — functionally similar to ibuprofen — addressing chronic inflammation directly. Oleic acid activates SIRT1. Polyphenols modulate NF-κB inflammatory signaling. The Mediterranean diet's longevity effect is substantially attributable to EVOO.

Hallmarks addressed: Chronic inflammation (11), altered intercellular communication (10), deregulated nutrient sensing (6), dysbiosis (12).

DOSE: 2–4 tablespoons daily · RAW preferred — heat degrades polyphenols
QUALITY: First cold press, <0.8% acidity, harvest date on label
MECHANISM: Oleocanthal → COX inhibition · Oleic acid → SIRT1 activation · Hydroxytyrosol → antioxidant + anti-inflammatory
WILD FATTY FISH (SARDINES · SALMON · MACKEREL)
OMEGA-3 SOURCE · COMPLETE PROTEIN · VITAMIN D
STRONG EVIDENCE

Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA and DHA) are the most studied anti-aging nutrients after vitamins. The American Heart Association's science advisory confirms cardiovascular protection. Multiple meta-analyses associate higher omega-3 intake with longer telomere length — directly addressing Hallmark 2. DHA is the primary structural fat of brain tissue. Sardines specifically provide bone-available calcium from edible bones plus CoQ10.

Hallmarks addressed: Telomere attrition (2), chronic inflammation (11), mitochondrial dysfunction (7), altered intercellular communication (10).

DOSE: 3–4 servings per week minimum
SARDINES: Best value — omega-3 dense, low mercury, CoQ10 source
MECHANISM: EPA/DHA → anti-inflammatory eicosanoids · DHA → membrane fluidity · EPA → telomerase activity support
DARK BERRIES (BLUEBERRY · BLACKBERRY · POMEGRANATE)
POLYPHENOL MATRIX · ANTHOCYANIN SOURCE · UROLITHIN PRECURSOR
STRONG EVIDENCE

Berries address aging through multiple simultaneous mechanisms. Anthocyanins cross the blood-brain barrier — rare for dietary compounds — and provide direct neuroprotection. Pomegranate specifically contains ellagitannins that gut bacteria convert to urolithin A, one of the most promising longevity compounds currently in clinical trials. The conversion requires a healthy microbiome — another reason gut health is foundational.

Hallmarks addressed: Genomic instability (1), chronic inflammation (11), dysbiosis (12), mitochondrial dysfunction (7 — via urolithin A precursors).

DOSE: 1 cup daily · Frozen is equivalent to fresh in polyphenol content
POMEGRANATE: Key source of ellagitannins → urolithin A conversion (microbiome dependent)
MECHANISM: Anthocyanins → NF-κB inhibition · Pterostilbene → SIRT1 activation · Ellagic acid → DNA protection
CRUCIFEROUS VEGETABLES (BROCCOLI · BRUSSELS · CABBAGE)
SULFORAPHANE SOURCE · DETOXIFICATION · NRF2 ACTIVATOR
STRONG EVIDENCE

Sulforaphane — released when cruciferous vegetables are chewed or chopped — is one of the most potent known activators of the NRF2 pathway, the master regulator of cellular antioxidant defense. NRF2 activation upregulates over 200 cytoprotective genes simultaneously. Cruciferous vegetables also contain DIM (diindolylmethane) which supports healthy estrogen metabolism and glucosinolates that activate liver detoxification enzymes.

Hallmarks addressed: Genomic instability (1), loss of proteostasis (4), cellular senescence (8), chronic inflammation (11).

DOSE: 1–2 cups daily · Chop and wait 10 minutes before cooking — activates myrosinase enzyme
KEY COMPOUND: Sulforaphane → NRF2 → 200+ cytoprotective genes
PREPARATION: Light steam preserves sulforaphane · Boiling destroys it
LEAFY GREENS (SPINACH · KALE · ARUGULA)
FOLATE · VITAMIN K1 · LUTEIN · NITRATES
STRONG EVIDENCE

Folate is a methyl donor essential for DNA methylation — the epigenetic mechanism most directly linked to biological aging clocks. Inadequate folate accelerates epigenetic aging measurably. Vitamin K1 in greens converts to K2 MK-4 in tissues, directing calcium into bones and away from arteries. Dietary nitrates from greens convert to nitric oxide, preserving vascular function that declines with age.

Hallmarks addressed: Epigenetic alterations (3), genomic instability (1), altered intercellular communication (10).

DOSE: 2+ cups daily · Raw or lightly cooked
FOLATE: Critical for DNA methylation — epigenetic aging clock intervention
NOTE: Arugula highest nitrate content · Kale highest K1 content
WALNUTS & MIXED NUTS
ALA OMEGA-3 · POLYPHENOLS · HEALTHY FAT MATRIX
STRONG EVIDENCE

Walnuts contain the highest polyphenol content of any nut and uniquely provide ALA (alpha-linolenic acid), the plant precursor to EPA and DHA. The WAHA trial demonstrated that daily walnut consumption reduces inflammatory markers and improves cognitive performance in older adults. Nuts as a category are one of the strongest dietary predictors of longevity in epidemiological data — 19% reduction in all-cause mortality in the highest quintile of consumption.

Hallmarks addressed: Chronic inflammation (11), telomere attrition (2), dysbiosis (12), altered intercellular communication (10).

DOSE: 1 oz (28g) daily · Walnuts preferred for ALA content
ALL-CAUSE MORTALITY: 19% reduction in highest consumption quintile
MECHANISM: ALA → partial EPA/DHA conversion · Ellagitannins → gut microbiome support
LEGUMES (LENTILS · BLACK BEANS · CHICKPEAS)
FIBER MATRIX · PLANT PROTEIN · RESISTANT STARCH
STRONG EVIDENCE

Legumes are the single food most consistently associated with longevity across Blue Zone populations — the five regions with the highest concentrations of centenarians. The mechanism is primarily gut-mediated: legume fiber ferments to short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs), particularly butyrate, which maintains intestinal barrier integrity, reduces systemic inflammation, and directly inhibits histone deacetylases — an epigenetic anti-aging mechanism.

Hallmarks addressed: Dysbiosis (12), epigenetic alterations (3), chronic inflammation (11), deregulated nutrient sensing (6).

DOSE: 1/2 cup cooked daily minimum
BLUE ZONE DATA: Most consistent longevity food across all five populations
MECHANISM: Fiber → butyrate → HDAC inhibition (epigenetic) + barrier integrity
GREEN TEA (MATCHA PREFERRED)
EGCG · L-THEANINE · CATECHIN MATRIX
MODERATE EVIDENCE

EGCG (epigallocatechin gallate) is one of the most studied polyphenols in longevity research. It inhibits DNMT (DNA methyltransferase), modulates mTOR, activates AMPK, and has demonstrated senolytic properties in cell studies. Japanese population data shows consistent association between green tea consumption and reduced all-cause mortality. L-theanine provides neuroprotection and reduces cortisol — addressing the stress-aging connection.

Hallmarks addressed: Epigenetic alterations (3), deregulated nutrient sensing (6), cellular senescence (8), chronic inflammation (11).

DOSE: 2–4 cups daily · Matcha provides full leaf catechins
EGCG: mTOR inhibition · AMPK activation · senolytic properties (cell studies)
HONEST NOTE: Most evidence is observational or in vitro. RCT data in humans is moderate.
FERMENTED FOODS (KEFIR · KIMCHI · SAUERKRAUT)
LIVE CULTURES · POSTBIOTICS · MICROBIOME ARCHITECTURE
MODERATE EVIDENCE

A 2021 Stanford Cell study found that a high-fermented food diet increased microbiome diversity and decreased 19 inflammatory markers in healthy adults — more effectively than a high-fiber diet alone. Microbiome diversity is one of the strongest predictors of healthy aging and is measurably lower in centenarian populations that age poorly versus those that age well. Fermented foods address Hallmark 12 more directly than any supplement.

Hallmarks addressed: Dysbiosis (12), chronic inflammation (11), altered intercellular communication (10).

DOSE: 1–2 servings daily · Variety matters more than volume
STANFORD DATA: 19 inflammatory markers reduced vs high-fiber alone
KEFIR: Highest live culture density · postbiotic production · GABA synthesis
DARK CHOCOLATE (85%+ CACAO)
FLAVANOL SOURCE · NITRIC OXIDE · MAGNESIUM
MODERATE EVIDENCE

Cacao flavanols — specifically epicatechin — activate nitric oxide synthase, improving endothelial function measurably within hours of consumption. The COSMOS-Mind trial (2021) showed that cocoa flavanol supplementation slowed cognitive aging by 3 years in a large RCT. Magnesium in dark chocolate supports over 300 enzymatic reactions including DNA repair. At 85%+ the sugar content is minimal and the flavanol content is maximized.

Hallmarks addressed: Altered intercellular communication (10), chronic inflammation (11), genomic instability (1).

DOSE: 20–30g daily (85%+ cacao)
COSMOS-MIND: 3-year cognitive aging reduction in RCT
MECHANISM: Epicatechin → NO synthase → vascular function · Magnesium → DNA repair cofactor
MUSHROOMS (SHIITAKE · LION'S MANE · REISHI)
BETA-GLUCANS · ERGOTHIONEINE · NGF STIMULATORS
MODERATE EVIDENCE

Ergothioneine — a unique antioxidant found almost exclusively in mushrooms — has been proposed as a longevity vitamin. It accumulates in mitochondria and nuclei where oxidative damage is highest. Lion's mane contains hericenones that stimulate Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) — the only dietary source of NGF stimulation with clinical human evidence. Beta-glucans from all mushrooms modulate innate immunity and gut microbiome architecture.

Hallmarks addressed: Mitochondrial dysfunction (7), genomic instability (1), dysbiosis (12), stem cell exhaustion (9).

DOSE: 1–2 cups cooked daily · Dried mushrooms retain full ergothioneine
ERGOTHIONEINE: Proposed longevity vitamin — mitochondrial + nuclear antioxidant
LION'S MANE: NGF stimulation — only dietary source with clinical evidence
EGGS (WHOLE, PASTURE-RAISED)
CHOLINE · COMPLETE PROTEIN · FAT-SOLUBLE VITAMINS
MODERATE EVIDENCE

Choline is the most underconsumed essential nutrient in Western diets and a critical methyl donor for DNA methylation — directly addressing epigenetic aging. Over 90% of Americans don't meet adequate choline intake. Eggs are the most bioavailable dietary choline source. The long-standing concern about dietary cholesterol from eggs has been substantially revised — current evidence shows eggs do not meaningfully increase cardiovascular risk in healthy individuals.

Hallmarks addressed: Epigenetic alterations (3), genomic instability (1), stem cell exhaustion (9).

DOSE: 1–2 daily · Whole egg — yolk contains choline
CHOLINE: Critical methyl donor · >90% Americans deficient · Epigenetic aging intervention
HONEST NOTE: Cholesterol concern revised — current evidence does not support restriction in healthy individuals
TURMERIC (WITH BLACK PEPPER)
CURCUMIN · NF-κB INHIBITOR · SENOLYTIC
MODERATE EVIDENCE

Curcumin inhibits NF-κB — the master transcription factor of inflammation — with a potency comparable to some pharmaceutical anti-inflammatories. It also has demonstrated senolytic activity in cell studies, selectively eliminating senescent cells. The critical constraint: curcumin bioavailability is extremely low without piperine (black pepper). Combined with piperine, absorption increases 2000%. Without it, negligible amounts reach circulation.

Hallmarks addressed: Chronic inflammation (11), cellular senescence (8), deregulated nutrient sensing (6).

DOSE: 1/2 tsp daily with black pepper and fat
PIPERINE: 2000% absorption increase — non-negotiable
HONEST NOTE: Most dramatic effects in humans with existing inflammation. Prevention evidence is moderate.
GARLIC (RAW OR AGED)
ALLICIN · S-ALLYLCYSTEINE · ORGANOSULFUR COMPOUNDS
MODERATE EVIDENCE

Allicin — released when garlic is crushed or chopped and allowed to sit 10 minutes — is a potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and cardiovascular protective compound. Aged Black Garlic has undergone fermentation that converts allicin to more stable S-allylcysteine, which has superior bioavailability and has demonstrated telomere-protective effects in human studies. Garlic also acts as a prebiotic, selectively feeding Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium species.

Hallmarks addressed: Telomere attrition (2), chronic inflammation (11), dysbiosis (12).

DOSE: 2–4 cloves daily · Crush and wait 10 min before cooking
BLACK GARLIC: Superior bioavailability · telomere-protective S-allylcysteine
MECHANISM: Allicin → NF-κB inhibition · Prebiotic fiber → Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium
GREEN VEGETABLES (ASPARAGUS · AVOCADO · EDAMAME)
SPERMIDINE SOURCE · FOLATE · HEALTHY FAT
MODERATE EVIDENCE

This category earns its place primarily as the leading dietary source of spermidine — a polyamine that induces autophagy through a mechanism independent of mTOR. Autophagy is the cellular recycling process that declines with age and directly addresses Hallmarks 4 and 5. Wheat germ is the highest dietary spermidine source but these vegetables provide meaningful amounts alongside folate and healthy fat. Avocado provides oleic acid with similar SIRT1 activation to EVOO.

Hallmarks addressed: Loss of proteostasis (4), disabled macroautophagy (5), epigenetic alterations (3).

SPERMIDINE CONTENT: Wheat germ > aged cheese > soy > mushrooms > asparagus > avocado
MECHANISM: Spermidine → autophagy induction (mTOR-independent) → proteostasis maintenance
DOSE: Daily variety across category preferred
§ 03 · SUPPLEMENT ANALYSIS

The Supplement Architecture

What the evidence actually supports — graded honestly, mechanisms named
FORENSIC POSITION ON SUPPLEMENTS
Supplements address gaps in diet and target specific hallmarks that food alone cannot adequately reach. They do not replace the food architecture. Several compounds below have strong animal data and emerging human data — this does not make them equivalent to compounds with completed large-scale RCTs. The evidence grade is the honest signal. Act accordingly.
VITAMIN D3 + K2
FOUNDATIONAL · HORMONE PRECURSOR · CALCIUM DIRECTOR
STRONG EVIDENCE

Vitamin D3 functions as a hormone, not a vitamin — it regulates over 1000 genes. Deficiency is documented in over 40% of adults and directly accelerates immune senescence, bone loss, and cardiovascular aging. The Stanford Center on Longevity places D3 among its foundational supplements. K2 (MK-7 form) is critical pairing: D3 increases calcium absorption, K2 ensures that calcium goes to bones and teeth rather than arterial walls. Taking D3 without K2 risks arterial calcification.

Hallmarks addressed: Cellular senescence (8), altered intercellular communication (10), genomic instability (1).

DOSE D3: 2000–5000 IU daily with fat · Test serum levels — target 40–60 ng/mL
DOSE K2: 100–200 mcg MK-7 form daily
NON-NEGOTIABLE PAIRING: D3 without K2 risks arterial calcification
OMEGA-3 (EPA + DHA)
FOUNDATIONAL · ANTI-INFLAMMATORY · TELOMERE PROTECTIVE
STRONG EVIDENCE

If fish consumption is insufficient (under 3 servings per week), supplemental omega-3 is among the most evidence-backed interventions in aging science. The VITAL trial — 25,000 participants over 5 years — demonstrated meaningful cardiovascular and cancer risk reduction. Multiple RCTs confirm telomere length association. Target: omega-3 index above 8% (most Americans are below 4%).

Hallmarks addressed: Telomere attrition (2), chronic inflammation (11), mitochondrial dysfunction (7).

DOSE: 2–4g combined EPA+DHA daily · Fish oil or algae-derived
OMEGA-3 INDEX TARGET: >8% · Most Americans below 4%
FORM: Triglyceride form absorbs better than ethyl ester · Take with fat-containing meal
MAGNESIUM GLYCINATE
FOUNDATIONAL · DNA REPAIR COFACTOR · SLEEP ARCHITECTURE
STRONG EVIDENCE

Magnesium is a cofactor in over 300 enzymatic reactions including DNA repair, protein synthesis, and energy production. Over 50% of adults are deficient — soil depletion has reduced dietary magnesium significantly over the past 50 years. Magnesium deficiency directly accelerates DNA damage accumulation. It is also essential for sleep architecture — deep sleep is when the glymphatic system clears neurotoxic waste, making magnesium's sleep role an anti-aging function, not a comfort function.

Hallmarks addressed: Genomic instability (1), mitochondrial dysfunction (7), loss of proteostasis (4).

DOSE: 300–400mg glycinate form at night
GLYCINATE FORM: Highest absorption · No laxative effect unlike oxide
TIMING: Evening — supports sleep architecture and nocturnal DNA repair
NMN (NICOTINAMIDE MONONUCLEOTIDE)
NAD+ PRECURSOR · SIRTUIN ACTIVATOR · MITOCHONDRIAL SUPPORT
EMERGING EVIDENCE

NAD+ declines 50% between age 40 and 60. NAD+ is a cofactor for sirtuins (SIRT1–SIRT7) — the longevity proteins that regulate DNA repair, epigenetic maintenance, mitochondrial function, and metabolic homeostasis simultaneously. NMN is the direct precursor to NAD+ with superior bioavailability over direct NAD+ supplementation. Multiple completed human RCTs confirm NMN raises NAD+ levels in blood. Whether higher NAD+ translates to measurable aging deceleration in humans at scale remains an open question — the mechanistic case is compelling, the long-term human outcome data is not yet complete.

Hallmarks addressed: Mitochondrial dysfunction (7), genomic instability (1), epigenetic alterations (3), deregulated nutrient sensing (6).

DOSE: 250–500mg daily · Morning on empty stomach
NMN vs NR: NMN is direct NAD+ precursor · NR requires additional conversion step
HONEST STATUS: NAD+ elevation confirmed in humans. Longevity outcome translation: emerging, not confirmed.
UROLITHIN A
MITOPHAGY INDUCER · MUSCLE PRESERVATION · MITOCHONDRIAL QUALITY CONTROL
EMERGING EVIDENCE

Urolithin A is produced by gut bacteria from pomegranate ellagitannins — but only in individuals with the right microbiome composition (approximately 30–40% of the population can produce meaningful amounts). It is the most potent known nutritional inducer of mitophagy — selective autophagy of damaged mitochondria — the process by which cells recycle dysfunctional mitochondria and replace them with healthy ones. A 2022 Nature Aging RCT confirmed improved mitochondrial health markers in older adults. 2025 data shows cardiac mitochondrial preservation.

Hallmarks addressed: Mitochondrial dysfunction (7), disabled macroautophagy (5), loss of proteostasis (4), cellular senescence (8).

DOSE: 500mg–1g daily (supplement) · Or consume pomegranate + healthy microbiome
PRODUCTION: Only ~30–40% of people produce it from diet alone
2022 RCT: Nature Aging — confirmed mitochondrial health improvement in humans
SPERMIDINE
AUTOPHAGY INDUCER · EPIGENETIC REGULATOR · mTOR-INDEPENDENT
EMERGING EVIDENCE

Spermidine induces autophagy through a mechanism completely independent of mTOR — meaning it works synergistically with caloric restriction rather than redundantly. It also has direct epigenetic effects, regulating hypusination of eIF5A (a translation factor involved in longevity). Population studies show an inverse association between dietary spermidine intake and all-cause mortality. Spermidine levels decline with age and can be partially restored through diet (wheat germ, aged cheese, legumes) or supplementation.

Hallmarks addressed: Disabled macroautophagy (5), loss of proteostasis (4), epigenetic alterations (3).

DOSE: 1–5mg daily supplement · Or via wheat germ (highest dietary source)
MECHANISM: mTOR-independent autophagy induction · eIF5A hypusination
POPULATION DATA: Inverse association with all-cause mortality in observational studies
QUERCETIN + FISETIN
SENOLYTICS · SIRT1 ACTIVATORS · SENESCENT CELL CLEARANCE
EMERGING EVIDENCE

Senolytics selectively eliminate senescent cells — the "zombie cells" that accumulate with age and drive the SASP (senescence-associated secretory phenotype), a major source of chronic inflammation. Quercetin and fisetin are the most studied dietary senolytics. Mayo Clinic trials have tested quercetin + dasatinib combinations in humans. Fisetin showed a 25–30% reduction in senescent cells in mice with lifespan extension. Human RCT data is early but promising. Both are flavonoids found in foods but at concentrations too low to achieve senolytic dosing through diet alone.

Hallmarks addressed: Cellular senescence (8), chronic inflammation (11), deregulated nutrient sensing (6).

QUERCETIN DOSE: 500–1000mg with bromelain for absorption · 2–3x weekly (not daily)
FISETIN DOSE: 100–500mg · Intermittent dosing protocol (pulse not continuous)
HONEST STATUS: Senolytic mechanism confirmed in animals. Human outcome RCTs: ongoing.
RESVERATROL (+ TMG)
SIRT1 ACTIVATOR · AMPK MODULATOR · EPIGENETIC
EMERGING EVIDENCE

Resveratrol activates SIRT1 directly and potentiates the effects of NAD+ precursors — the combination is synergistic. David Sinclair's longevity protocol pairs NMN with resveratrol for this reason. The honest constraint: resveratrol bioavailability is notoriously poor. Trans-resveratrol taken with fat improves absorption significantly. TMG (trimethylglycine) is increasingly paired with resveratrol and NMN as a methyl donor to offset the methyl-consuming nature of elevated sirtuin activity.

Hallmarks addressed: Deregulated nutrient sensing (6), epigenetic alterations (3), mitochondrial dysfunction (7).

DOSE: 500mg–1g trans-resveratrol with fat · Morning with NMN
TMG PAIRING: 500mg–1g TMG — offsets methyl consumption from SIRT activation
HONEST STATUS: Strong animal and mechanistic data. Human RCT longevity outcomes: not yet confirmed.
ASHWAGANDHA (KSM-66)
ADAPTOGEN · CORTISOL MODULATOR · TELOMERE PROTECTIVE
MODERATE EVIDENCE

Chronic cortisol elevation is one of the most consistent accelerators of biological aging — it directly shortens telomeres, impairs immune function, and disrupts sleep architecture. Ashwagandha (KSM-66 extract) is the most studied adaptogen with the most consistent cortisol reduction data across multiple RCTs. A 2021 JAMA Network Open study showed significant thyroid and cortisol modulation. The longevity mechanism is primarily through stress axis regulation — addressing the upstream driver of multiple hallmarks simultaneously.

Hallmarks addressed: Telomere attrition (2), altered intercellular communication (10), cellular senescence (8).

DOSE: 300–600mg KSM-66 extract daily · Evening preferred
CORTISOL REDUCTION: Consistent across multiple RCTs — 15–30% reduction
MECHANISM: HPA axis modulation → cortisol reduction → telomere + immune preservation
CREATINE MONOHYDRATE
ATP BUFFER · COGNITIVE SUPPORT · MUSCLE PRESERVATION
STRONG EVIDENCE

Creatine is the most studied sports supplement with extensive safety data and an expanding longevity evidence base. Beyond muscle preservation — sarcopenia being a major aging risk factor — creatine has recently demonstrated cognitive benefits in aging adults. A 2023 meta-analysis found significant improvement in memory tasks in adults over 60. Creatine supplementation is particularly important for those with limited meat intake as dietary creatine comes primarily from animal protein.

Hallmarks addressed: Stem cell exhaustion (9), mitochondrial dysfunction (7), altered intercellular communication (10).

DOSE: 3–5g daily monohydrate · No loading phase necessary
COGNITIVE DATA: 2023 meta-analysis — significant memory improvement in adults 60+
SAFETY: Most extensively studied supplement in existence. 30+ years of safety data.
LION'S MANE MUSHROOM EXTRACT
NGF STIMULATOR · NEUROGENESIS · MYELIN SUPPORT
EMERGING EVIDENCE

Lion's mane is the only dietary source with clinical evidence of nerve growth factor (NGF) stimulation in humans. NGF supports neurogenesis, synaptic plasticity, and myelin maintenance — all of which decline with age. A landmark Japanese RCT showed significant cognitive improvement in mild cognitive impairment with lion's mane supplementation. A 2023 University of Queensland study identified specific compounds (hericene A) that directly stimulate hippocampal neuron growth. The neurological aging application has the strongest current evidence base.

Hallmarks addressed: Stem cell exhaustion (9), altered intercellular communication (10), chronic inflammation (11).

DOSE: 500mg–1g dual extract (water + alcohol) daily
DUAL EXTRACT: Required — water extract for beta-glucans, alcohol extract for hericenones
UQ 2023: Hericene A directly stimulates hippocampal neuron growth
COLLAGEN PEPTIDES + VITAMIN C
EXTRACELLULAR MATRIX · SKIN · JOINT · VASCULAR INTEGRITY
MODERATE EVIDENCE

Collagen synthesis declines approximately 1% per year after age 25. The extracellular matrix — the structural scaffold between cells — degrades with age and directly drives skin, joint, vascular, and organ aging. Collagen peptide supplementation with vitamin C (the cofactor for collagen synthesis) has demonstrated skin elasticity improvement and joint pain reduction in multiple RCTs. The combination is required — vitamin C alone without amino acid precursors doesn't address the raw material deficit.

Hallmarks addressed: Altered intercellular communication (10), cellular senescence (8).

DOSE COLLAGEN: 10–15g hydrolyzed peptides daily · On empty stomach or with vitamin C
DOSE VITAMIN C: 500mg–1g with collagen · Cofactor for hydroxylation reactions
TIMING: Morning fasted for maximum absorption
