SURFACE LAYER · 0m
Buoy Array + Solar Sheets

Floating hexagonal array. Buoys are uncoated textured concrete — deliberately rough for mussel spat settlement. No antifouling paint. No cleaning schedule. The hull is the substrate.

Flexible thin-film photovoltaic sheets float between buoys like lily pads. Tethered loosely — rising and falling with swell rather than resisting it. Creates shaded microclimate beneath: nursery roof for juvenile bivalves and kelp sporelings.

WATER COLUMN · 0–20m
Tether Farm

0–3m: Mussel lines in highest-flow zone. Buoy motion aerates water. Filter feeders at depth of maximum phytoplankton.

3–10m: Kelp longlines. Absorb dissolved N and P — metabolic waste from mussels above, agricultural runoff from land. Wave attenuation as co-function (verified: AQUABREAK).

10m–seafloor: Bare tether colonized by sponges, anemones, deep-water bivalves. Becomes vertical reef without seeding.

SEAFLOOR · Anchor
Reef Modules

Gravity anchors with deliberately complex shapes — cavities, overhangs, varied substrate textures. Designed as artificial reef, not just ballast.

Additional reef modules in array exclusion zone. No trawling. No dredging. The energy array's legal footprint becomes de facto marine protected area.

Place the buoys. The flow slows. The mussels arrive. The kelp colonizes. The fish aggregate. The reef grows. The water cleans. The energy flows. The beaver places one dam. The rest is not the beaver's work.
§ 02 · THE ORGANS

Emergent Functions · What the Geometry Makes Inevitable

These are not separately designed. They emerge from the skeleton because the geometry changed the physical environment.
FILTRATION FIRST

Bivalves clean the water column. Not farmed — filtering. Population controlled by substrate availability, not harvest quota. Surplus harvested when carrying capacity reached.

WAVE ATTENUATION

Kelp performs dual function: crop + coastal protection. Verified by AQUABREAK — up to 40% wave energy reduction at peak wave periods. Creates calmer interior water for spat settlement.

DISTRIBUTED ENERGY

Buoy motion drives piezoelectric strips along tether attachments. No onboard generator. No single point of failure. Solar sheets supplement. Hybrid output powers operations with surplus to grid.

HABITAT EMERGENCE

Fish aggregate around floating objects and reef structures. Lobster, octopus, rockfish arrive without stocking. PNNL verified: WECs function as FADs and artificial reefs with high secondary production.

WATER PURIFICATION

Bivalves filter particulates. Kelp absorbs dissolved nutrients. Slowed flow increases residence time. Heavy metals sequestered. Same mechanism as beaver ponds — verified by ISU nitrate studies.

FUTURE SITE VALUE

Over years: scuba diving destination, scientific documentation site, possible marine preservation area. The array becomes a destination, not infrastructure. Value accrues without extraction.

§ 04 · OPEN NODES

What Remains Unverified

Honestly named. Held, not dismissed. The architecture doesn't require these to close — they affect operations, not geometry.
OPEN NODE 01 — HARVEST LOGISTICS
Bivalve settlement on buoy hulls is verified. Optimal harvest vessel design, rotation scheduling, and processing chain for buoy-scraped mussels at commercial scale is not established. The Swedish project proved biology works; the operational economics are unmodeled.
OPEN NODE 02 — PASSIVE PV CLEANING
Sediment accumulation on flexible PV causes 0.5% efficiency loss per 45 days. Whether wave motion alone can keep panels sufficiently clean, or whether active cleaning is required, is unverified in open-ocean deployment.
OPEN NODE 03 — UNIFIED PERMITTING
EU framework exists. U.S. framework (BOEM, Army Corps, NOAA) is fragmented across agencies. No single permit currently covers a structure that is simultaneously a power plant, aquaculture facility, and marine protected area.
OPEN NODE 04 — FULL INTEGRATION DEMONSTRATION
Wave + solar + bivalves + kelp + reef has not been deployed as a single integrated unit cell. Every pairwise integration is verified. The full five-function compression exists architecturally but not yet as an operational prototype.
